## 1. What is Phishing?
[cite_start]Phishing is a social engineering attack where malicious actors send deceptive messages to trick users into revealing sensitive information or downloading malicious software[cite: 32, 36].

## 2. How to Recognize Phishing Emails
[cite_start]Look out for these common warning signs[cite: 33]:
* **Urgent Language:** Demands immediate action (e.g., "Account suspended! Act now!").
* **Suspicious Sender Address:** The email looks official but the actual domain address is slightly misspelled or mismatched.
* **Generic Greetings:** Using "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name.
* **Mismatched Links:** Hovering over a link reveals a completely different web address than what is displayed.

---

## 3. Real-World Example Scenario
* **The Bait:** An email claiming to be from your bank states your debit card has been blocked due to suspicious activity.
* [cite_start]**The Trap:** It contains a link leading to a fake website that looks identical to your bank login portal[cite: 33].
* [cite_start]**The Goal:** To steal your login credentials and password headers when you attempt to log in[cite: 33].

---

## 4. Best Practices to Stay Safe
* [cite_start]**Double-Check URLs:** Always look closely at the browser address bar before typing passwords[cite: 33].
* **Never Click Direct Links:** If a service alerts you, navigate directly to their official website instead of clicking email links.
* **Use MFA:** Enable Multi-Factor Authentication so attackers cannot log in even if they steal your password.
